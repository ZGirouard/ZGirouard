About Me

My interest in software began during my senior year in high school. I found myself browsing Stack Overflow and numerous websites during my "Intro to Java" class, teaching myself material that wasn't covered.

Currently, I'm a second-year student at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln double majoring in Computer Science and Sociology. I've always been interested in the intersection between people and computer-interaction, and my majors have allowed me to explore this. Additionally, I'm a propenent of sustainability and accessibility when it comes to software development.

I'm also the VP for Internal Relations for Intialize, a volunteer club that teaches middle schoolers in the Lincoln school system to create their own websites. Furthermore, I participate in the UNL dodgeball club, which is consistently ranked top 10 in the NCDA.

In my free time, you can find me listening to music, going to concerts or art muesems, and skateboarding. Outside of my creative endeavors, I love thrift shoppping, lifting weights, and hitting up my local record store.
